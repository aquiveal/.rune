@Domain
These rules MUST trigger when the AI is tasked with planning user research, structuring a card sorting activity, generating user recruitment plans, drafting participant invitations, defining testing sample sizes, or creating facilitation guides for user research sessions.

@Vocabulary
- **Facilitator**: The individual managing the card sort. Must be organized, a multitasker, personable, a good listener, and knowledgeable about the domain content.
- **End Users**: The actual target audience for the system being designed. The primary and most valuable participants for a card sort.
- **Stakeholder/Client Sort**: Card sorts conducted with internal team members, managers, or clients. Used primarily for consensus building or communication, NOT for primary data.
- **Team Sort**: A collaborative card sorting session. The optimal configuration is exactly three (3) participants who share equal standing.
- **Power Dynamics Anti-Pattern**: The detrimental mixing of managers with their staff, or IT staff with non-IT staff, which artificially skews the results due to deference or intimidation.
- **Icebreaker**: A mandatory preliminary activity used when team sort participants do not previously know one another.
- **Sample Size Thresholds**: The specific number of required participants based on the goal (e.g., 5-6 for broad ideas; 10+ per segment for detailed comparisons).
- **Statistical Significance**: An academic concept explicitly defined as irrelevant and unnecessary for information architecture and card sorting projects.

@Objectives
- Define the optimal facilitator profile and required preparation routines.
- Accurately target, segment, and recruit the correct end-users for the domain context.
- Structure team dynamics to eliminate power imbalances and maximize collaborative insight.
- Determine precise, practical participant volumes based strictly on research goals rather than academic statistical formulas.
- Draft recruitment communications that are personal, transparent, and strictly avoid spam.

@Guidelines

**Facilitator Definition & Preparation**
- The AI MUST define the facilitator's role as requiring five traits: highly organized (managing timing and data), able to multitask (taking notes while observing and anticipating questions), personable (relaxing participants), a good listener (remaining quiet, not centralizing attention), and knowledgeable (able to clarify card content).
- The AI MUST require a test run or practice session for the facilitator (with family, colleagues, or an experienced mentor) to anticipate flow and questions.

**Participant Selection & Segmentation**
- The AI MUST target "End Users" as the primary participants. They must have a vested interest in, and understanding of, the content.
- The AI MUST strictly isolate data gathered from stakeholders, clients, or internal managers from the end-user data. Stakeholder data is for communication and consensus, not architectural truth.

**Team Structuring & Group Dynamics**
- The AI MUST restrict recommended team sizes to exactly three (3) participants. The AI MUST explicitly reject team sizes of four or five, as these lead to passive, non-contributing members.
- The AI MUST enforce strict peer-level groupings. The AI MUST NOT combine managers with subordinate staff. The AI MUST NOT combine IT personnel with non-IT personnel.
- If team participants do not know each other, the AI MUST schedule an icebreaker, product discussion, or practice sort prior to the main activity.

**Participant Volume (Sample Sizing)**
- The AI MUST NOT set participant quotas based on "Statistical Significance."
- For learning broad ideas or checking if a project is on track: The AI MUST specify 5 to 6 teams or individual sorts.
- For exploring ideas in detail: The AI MUST specify starting with 10 participants/teams, running preliminary analysis, and stopping if a consistent pattern emerges.
- For comparing different user groups (e.g., audience types, demographics): The AI MUST specify recruiting enough participants *per group* to establish a baseline (starting at 10 per group) before comparing them.
- The AI MUST advise that if no patterns emerge after a reasonable number of sorts (e.g., 30), adding more participants will not fix the issue; the domain is simply too complex or the sort must be redesigned.

**Recruitment Methodologies**
- For Intranets: The AI MUST suggest internal networks, staff directories, or selection by role/work area.
- For Existing Websites: The AI MUST target existing core users and key stakeholders.
- For New/Public Sites (no existing base): The AI MUST suggest recruitment via market research firms, call-center/help-desk logs, website/newsletter ads, or conference/trade-show booths (with incentives).
- The AI MUST explicitly forbid the use of bulk, unsolicited email (spam) for recruitment.

**Invitation Structuring**
- The AI MUST ensure participant invitations contain exactly these six elements:
  1. What the project is about.
  2. What a card sort activity entails (e.g., sorting index cards into groups).
  3. How the card sort results will be used in the project.
  4. Who they will be working with (team context).
  5. The exact time commitment (e.g., less than an hour).
  6. Reassurance that no special knowledge or preparation is required, and there are no wrong answers.

@Workflow

1. **Define the Facilitation Plan**: Detail the required facilitator traits and mandate a test-run schedule to practice observation and note-taking.
2. **Determine the Participant Profile**: Identify the target end-users. If stakeholders are included, document a strict protocol to quarantine their data from the end-user data.
3. **Determine Participant Volume**: Evaluate the research goal (Broad ideas = 5-6; Comparison/Detail = 10 per segment) and define the exact participant quota. Exclude any references to p-values or statistical significance.
4. **Structure the Teams**: Form groups of exactly three (3) participants. Audit the groups to ensure no manager/staff or IT/non-IT overlaps exist. Inject icebreaker steps if participants are strangers.
5. **Formulate the Recruitment Strategy**: Select the recruitment channels based on the domain (intranet vs. public site). Add constraints prohibiting bulk spam.
6. **Draft the Invitation Script**: Write a personal, targeted invitation containing the six mandatory contextual elements.

@Examples (Do's and Don'ts)

**Principle: Team Sizing and Dynamics**
- [DO]: "We will assemble groups of exactly three participants for the team sorts. We have ensured that all three participants in Group A are peer-level staff members from the marketing department to avoid any deference to authority."
- [DON'T]: "To maximize efficiency, we will put five people in a single group, including the department director and an IT systems architect, alongside three junior copywriters."

**Principle: Participant Volume & Statistical Significance**
- [DO]: "Because our goal is to check if our draft architecture is on track, we will run 5 to 6 card sort sessions. This is sufficient to identify broad mental models and validate our assumptions."
- [DON'T]: "We need to recruit at least 150 participants for this card sort so that our groupings achieve a statistically significant p-value before we can proceed with the information architecture."

**Principle: Recruitment Communication**
- [DO]: "Hi [Name], we are redesigning the [Project] website to make information easier to find. We're running a 'card sort'—a simple activity where you group index cards into categories. Your input helps us organize the site. You'll work in a small team of three, it takes under an hour, and there is no preparation needed or right/wrong answers."
- [DON'T]: "URGENT: Click here to do a card sort for the new website. Tell us where the navigation links should go." (Fails to explain the activity, uses spam formatting, lacks time commitment, and fails to reassure the user about required knowledge).

**Principle: Handling Stakeholder Data**
- [DO]: "We will run a separate card sort with the client management team to build consensus and illustrate how their internal mental model differs from the actual end-users. We will keep their data in a separate analysis spreadsheet."
- [DON'T]: "We will merge the CEO's card sort results with the data from the 10 end-users to create the final hierarchical cluster."
