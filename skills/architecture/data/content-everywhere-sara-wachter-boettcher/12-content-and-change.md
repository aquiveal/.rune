# @Domain
Trigger these rules when executing tasks related to digital transformation planning, content strategy, organizational workflow design, client proposals, content governance, and user experience (UX) auditing. This applies to any request where the AI is tasked with structuring content systems, defining team roles, evaluating legacy content management workflows, or proposing content architectures for organizations or clients.

# @Vocabulary
- **Linchpin:** An individual or agent of change dedicated to helping an organization pivot toward practices that are human-centered, sustainable, and adaptable, moving away from business-as-usual.
- **Organizational Silos:** Isolated departments (e.g., IT, Marketing, PR) that fail to communicate or collaborate, resulting in disjointed, duplicative, or internally-focused content.
- **Customer-Centric:** An approach where content is structured and published based on the user's mental model and needs, rather than the organization's internal hierarchy or departmental divisions.
- **Organization-Centric (Business-Centric):** An anti-pattern where an organization prioritizes its own internal structure, short-term sales, or data collection needs over the user experience (e.g., spammy SEO, excessive data collection forms).
- **Clear Vision:** An organizational purpose that transcends basic revenue streams or specific products, providing a unified direction for content creation and collaboration.
- **Cross-Functional Team:** A collaborative group drawing representatives from multiple departments (e.g., IT, UX, Editorial) to guide digital initiatives and bypass bureaucratic silos.
- **Magic Bullet Pitch:** The anti-pattern of outside consultants or agencies selling a technical solution (like a new CMS) as an instant fix for content problems, rather than acknowledging the hard organizational change required.

# @Objectives
- Treat content strategy as a mechanism for organizational change, breaking down legacy silos and enforcing cross-departmental collaboration.
- Shift the focus of content architecture entirely away from the organization's internal structure and squarely onto the customer's needs and tasks.
- Ensure that technical content implementations (like APIs or CMSs) are accompanied by recommendations for cultural and workflow adaptations.
- For external consulting or agency tasks, prioritize honest, process-driven solutions over quick, superficial technical fixes.
- Identify and mitigate organizational fear and resistance to change, prioritizing human value over robotic or purely metric-driven content production.

# @Guidelines
- **Drive Organizational Change:** The AI MUST explicitly connect content restructuring to organizational workflow changes. Do not solve a content problem without addressing the human/workflow problem that caused it.
- **Enforce Customer-Centricity:** The AI MUST NOT replicate organizational charts in website navigation, taxonomy, or content architecture. Content MUST be abstracted away from the specific agency or department that produces it.
- **Establish Clear Vision:** When analyzing content needs, the AI MUST request or define a clear, user-focused vision. Reject "everyone is our customer" mentalities.
- **Promote Collaboration:** The AI MUST recommend cross-functional governance models. Do not assign content ownership to a single siloed department (e.g., never assign CMS architecture solely to IT without editorial input).
- **Reject Organization-First Tactics:** The AI MUST flag and remove manipulative tactics designed solely for organizational benefit at the expense of the user. This includes excessive personal data collection, forced opt-ins, dark patterns, and keyword-stuffing SEO.
- **Honest Consulting Practices:** When generating client proposals or agency recommendations, the AI MUST frame content strategy as a rigorous process of change, not a seamless software installation. Do not promise that a tool alone will fix messy content.
- **Filter Unready Clients:** The AI MUST include evaluation criteria for client readiness. If an organization is unwilling to break the status quo or collaborate, the AI MUST recommend walking away rather than providing a superficial fix.
- **Create Cross-Functional Task Forces:** When designing workflows, recommend an interdepartmental "innovation center" or task force (similar to the U.S. government's Digital Services Innovation Center) to bypass bureaucracy and establish repeatable, successful pilot projects.

# @Workflow
1. **Assess Organizational Readiness:** Evaluate the current state of the organization's workflows. Identify siloed departments, lack of shared vision, and organization-centric content practices.
2. **Define the Customer-Centric Vision:** Shift the focus. Define exactly what the content must do to help the user complete tasks or improve their life, ignoring internal departmental boundaries.
3. **Audit for Organization-First Anti-Patterns:** Scan existing content and workflows for spammy SEO, excessive data requests, and internally-driven navigation. Replace these with user-first alternatives.
4. **Design Cross-Functional Teams:** Establish a governance model that requires collaboration. Define roles that pull from IT, marketing, PR, and editorial to manage the content ecosystem collectively.
5. **Frame the Change Process (For Proposals):** If acting as an outside consultant, draft recommendations that explicitly state the hard work, training, and cultural shifts required. Strip out any language that sells a CMS or tool as a "magic bullet."
6. **Execute Pilot Projects:** Instead of attempting a top-down overhaul of a massive bureaucracy, architect small, cross-functional pilot projects that prove the value of structured, customer-centric content.

# @Examples (Do's and Don'ts)

**Principle: Customer-Centric Content Architecture**
- [DO]: Structure an educational financial portal around user tasks: "Apply for Aid," "Manage Loans," "Consolidate Debt."
- [DON'T]: Maintain 14 different websites or navigation nodes representing the 14 different government agencies or university departments that handle financial aid.

**Principle: Organizational Change & Consulting**
- [DO]: Write a project proposal that states: "Implementing this API requires a 6-month restructuring of the editorial workflow, cross-departmental training, and breaking down current publishing silos."
- [DON'T]: Write a project proposal that states: "We will install a new CMS that will automatically make your content future-ready and solve your publishing bottlenecks overnight."

**Principle: Organization-First Tactics vs. User Value**
- [DO]: Create high-quality, structured content hubs centered around a specific domain or user need to naturally earn search engine visibility.
- [DON'T]: Recommend writing dozens of low-quality, keyword-stuffed articles or creating fake link-bait to artificially inflate SEO rankings.

**Principle: Cross-Functional Collaboration**
- [DO]: Design a CMS implementation plan where content strategists, writers, and UX designers work directly with database developers to map the content model.
- [DON'T]: Allow the IT department to select, install, and configure the CMS fields based purely on database efficiency, forcing writers to adapt to an unintuitive interface later.
