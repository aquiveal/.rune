@Domain
This rule file MUST be triggered when the AI is analyzing, planning, auditing, designing, or developing content strategies, content lifecycles, omnichannel consumer experiences, content management systems (CMS), digital asset management (DAM) integrations, taxonomy, metadata structures, or cross-channel brand messaging.

@Vocabulary
- **Content**: Any information that someone records. This includes text, images, video, audio, PDFs, multimedia, rich media, social media, and metadata.
- **Digital Content**: Information created by someone and stored in a digital format with the capability of being shared (or not) in the future.
- **Consumer**: Anyone who consumes content, whether internal or external to an organization, through any platform. This term supersedes "end user."
- **Content Experience**: The emotional response and mental experience a consumer has when engaging with content. It encompasses every interaction with branded content across all distribution-exchange points.
- **Enterprise Content Strategy**: The systematic, thoughtful approach to surfacing the most relevant, effective, and appropriate content at the most opportune time, to the appropriate user, for the purpose of achieving a company’s strategic business objectives and its customers’ goals.
- **Content Delivery**: The processes used to acquire, create, review, publish, evaluate, optimize, maintain, and archive content.
- **Content Governance**: The operational and organizational processes that sustain the success of content.
- **Closed-Loop Process**: A cyclical lifecycle model where a content experience is never considered "complete." Measurements and continual evaluation inform future priorities.
- **Performance-Driven Content**: Content that is set up for evaluation and measurement so an organization can improve it continuously.
- **Future-Proofing**: Setting up a content ecosystem (processes, strategy, and structural models like taxonomy/tagging) to respond and adapt to new technology, changing user behaviors, and business changes, thereby minimizing rework.
- **Channel**: A platform used to deliver content (e.g., desktop, mobile tablet, in-store kiosk, print, TV).
- **Multichannel**: An approach indicating that an organization optimizes content for more than one channel, often treating each channel's experience independently.
- **Omnichannel**: An all-encompassing model that provides tailored content at every point in the customer journey across analog, digital, in-store, and person-to-person interactions.

@Objectives
- Treat all content, regardless of format, as a high-value business and organizational asset capable of generating definitive monetary or brand value.
- Shift the focus of content creation from merely "writing good content" to architecting the robust behind-the-scenes mechanisms (Experience, Delivery, Governance) required to support it.
- Design ecosystems based on a closed-loop, performance-driven lifecycle rather than a linear project timeline.
- Transition digital strategies from simple Multichannel distribution to holistic Omnichannel customer journeys.
- Safeguard the project lifecycle by ruthlessly mandating a comprehensive "Assess" (current-state) phase before any design or implementation begins.

@Guidelines
- **Terminology Constraint**: The AI MUST use the term "Consumer" instead of "End User" when discussing content audiences, as "end user" incorrectly implies active interaction beyond the passive consumption of content.
- **Strategy Triad Requirement**: Every enterprise content strategy generated or evaluated by the AI MUST explicitly address three core factors:
  1. Content Experience (defining the stories to be told and the consumer interaction).
  2. Content Delivery (the workflow of acquiring, creating, publishing, and archiving).
  3. Content Governance (the operational structures sustaining success).
- **Omnichannel vs. Multichannel**: The AI MUST explicitly differentiate between these two concepts. Do not recommend "Multichannel" (duplicating or optimizing content independently per platform) when "Omnichannel" (tailoring content to specific steps in a unified, cross-platform customer journey) is required.
- **Assess Phase Mandate**: The AI MUST categorically refuse to initiate or validate any "Design" or "Implement" activities if a thorough "Assess" phase (current-state assessment) has not been completed. Quick-and-dirty content inventories are strictly prohibited as they lead to hidden content, delayed schedules, and cost overruns.
- **Waterfall/Agile Agnosticism**: Whether the project uses Agile or Waterfall methodology, the AI MUST enforce that current-state assessments occur *prior* to kicking off design activities for any given deliverable.
- **Trigger Indicators**: The AI MUST inject enterprise content strategy best practices when the user mentions: unknown content volume, scattered content storage, homegrown publishing systems, unassigned content ownership, outdated search results, "never delete" policies, duplicate CMS entries, slow editorial changes, or personalization initiatives.
- **Future-Proofing Architecture**: When designing content models, the AI MUST recommend a dual framework: 1) A closed-loop sustainability process, and 2) A rigorous structural model for organizing, labeling, and tagging content.

@Workflow
To execute an Enterprise Content Strategy project, the AI MUST follow this rigid, 9-step closed-loop lifecycle:

1. **Plan**: Define high-level project parameters, identify team structures, establish budgets, and determine what the project will encompass.
2. **Assess**: Conduct an exhaustive survey of the current content ecosystem. 
   - Analyze business goals, content effectiveness, user feedback, analytics, competitive landscape, technology constraints, internal delivery processes, and governance evaluations.
3. **Define**: Synthesize Assess-phase inputs to frame the future project. Produce high-level goals, define the project scope, and establish detailed business requirements to secure budgets and inform technology needs.
4. **Design**: Architect the future-state experience. Determine exactly what content and information goes into the specific channels (e.g., website, mobile, user guides) based on omnichannel customer journeys.
5. **Implement (Build)**: Collaborate with technology teams to build/execute the design. Finalize the content and perform migration of existing content into the new system.
6. **Publish**: Distribute the content to the consumer. Include ongoing maintenance steps and the moderation of user-generated content.
7. **Measure**: Evaluate content performance using analytics. Assess what is consumed, shared, and commented on against the defined business goals.
8. **Optimize**: Review measurement findings to recommend improvements. Decide where to invest further, address new business needs, and identify weak points for optimization. Feed this data back into the lifecycle.
9. **Govern**: (Continuous Central Step) Establish and enforce a governance model. Implement standards, processes, organizational structures, and people to maintain the effectiveness of the entire closed-loop system.

@Examples (Do's and Don'ts)

**Principle: Multichannel vs. Omnichannel**
- [DO]: "We will map the customer's journey from seeing a TV ad, to researching on a tablet, to scanning a QR code in-store via a smartphone, ensuring the content is tailored for their specific goal at each touchpoint."
- [DON'T]: "We will create a multichannel strategy by taking our desktop website content and making it fit on a mobile screen and a printed brochure."

**Principle: Project Lifecycle Sequencing**
- [DO]: "Before we begin designing the new CMS taxonomy and page layouts, we must conduct a full assess phase, including stakeholder interviews and a comprehensive audit of all existing legacy content."
- [DON'T]: "Let's use an Agile approach and immediately start designing wireframes for the new website homepage, we can figure out what content we have later during development."

**Principle: Terminology and Audience Modeling**
- [DO]: "The user guide must be written for the content consumer, keeping in mind the emotional experience and mental response they will have when trying to resolve a product issue."
- [DON'T]: "This text is just for the end-user interacting with the interface."

**Principle: Content as a Business Asset**
- [DO]: "The metadata tags, YouTube videos, and PDF whitepapers are proprietary business assets that require a defined acquisition, review, and archival workflow."
- [DON'T]: "Content strategy is just about writing punchy copy; the IT team will handle the metadata and PDFs."
