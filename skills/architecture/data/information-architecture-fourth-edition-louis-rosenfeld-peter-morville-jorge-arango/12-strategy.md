# @Domain
Trigger these rules when the user requests assistance with Information Architecture (IA) strategy, conceptual framework development, transition from user research to design phases, drafting IA strategy reports, defining metadata/content management schemas, or creating user scenarios and conceptual metaphors for stakeholder presentations.

# @Vocabulary
*   **Information Architecture (IA) Strategy:** A high-level conceptual framework for structuring and organizing an information environment that provides direction and scope for design and implementation.
*   **TACT:** The four-step iterative strategy development process: Think, Articulate, Communicate, and Test.
*   **Strawman Work Product:** An explicitly informal, early-stage draft intended to provoke reactions, expose flaws, and jump-start discussion without triggering ego-defensiveness.
*   **Top-Down IA:** Defining the major organization schemes and labeling systems (the primary hierarchy).
*   **Bottom-Up IA:** Identifying document/object types and defining metadata schemas.
*   **Organizational Metaphors:** Leveraging a user's familiarity with a real-world system's organization (e.g., an auto dealership) to convey understanding of a digital system's organization.
*   **Functional Metaphors:** Connecting tasks performed in a traditional environment (e.g., browsing library shelves) to digital tasks.
*   **Visual Metaphors:** Using familiar graphic elements (e.g., yellow background for directory) to invoke real-world connections.
*   **Scenarios:** Narrative tools describing how distinct user types (personas) navigate and experience the product to excite stakeholders and explain the architecture.
*   **Rules, Roles, and Resources:** The three core components of effective content management. Rules are standardized workflows/guidelines; Roles are the staff types executing processes; Resources are the content itself and the CMS.
*   **Information Scent:** Cues (like second-level category labels) that help users understand the contents of a major category and predict navigation outcomes.

# @Objectives
*   Serve as the bridge between research (absorption/process) and design (creation/product).
*   Formulate a holistic IA strategy that balances business context, user needs, and content realities.
*   Proactively expose gaps in existing business strategies and content policies through the architectural process, rather than waiting for those strategies to be "finished."
*   Generate compelling communication deliverables (scenarios, metaphors, conceptual diagrams) that secure stakeholder buy-in.
*   Define comprehensive structural plans covering administration, technology integration, top-down hierarchies, and bottom-up metadata.

# @Guidelines
*   **Iterative Processing (TACT):** The AI MUST apply the TACT methodology. It MUST NOT present a strategy as a final, immutable artifact, but rather as an evolving framework subject to testing and stakeholder iteration.
*   **Handling the Chicken-and-Egg Problem:** The AI MUST NOT refuse to develop an IA strategy because a business plan or content repository is incomplete. Instead, the AI MUST use strategic IA questions to expose gaps and force decisions in business strategy and content policy.
*   **Required Strategy Elements:** Every comprehensive IA strategy generated by the AI MUST address:
    1.  *IA Administration:* Centralized (command-and-control) vs. decentralized (federated) management.
    2.  *Technology Integration:* CMS, search engines, auto-classification.
    3.  *Emphasis:* Clear designation of Top-Down vs. Bottom-Up focus.
    4.  *Top-Down Organization:* Primary hierarchy and labeling systems.
    5.  *Bottom-Up Content:* Document type identification.
    6.  *Metadata Definition:* Specification of administrative, structural, and descriptive fields (noting global vs. local scope).
    7.  *Navigation Systems:* Integrated and supplemental navigation leveraging both top-down and bottom-up structures.
*   **Metaphor Constraints:** The AI MUST use metaphors (Organizational, Functional, Visual) heavily during ideation and stakeholder presentations to communicate abstract ideas. However, the AI MUST NOT force literal metaphors into the final architecture if they become limiting (e.g., avoiding a literal "virtual town" where users must navigate a "post office" to send email).
*   **Scenario Generation:** The AI MUST draft scenarios that represent 3-4 distinct user types utilizing the environment in different modes (e.g., "investigative mode" vs. "serendipity mode").
*   **Content Management Definition:** When detailing content management in a strategy report, the AI MUST explicitly break recommendations down into Rules, Roles, and Resources.
*   **Testing Strategy:** The AI MUST recommend and design validation tests for the strategy, specifically specifying *closed card sorting* (to observe reactions to high-level organization) and *task performance analysis* (using stripped-down hierarchical menus or wireframes to isolate IA from visual design).

# @Workflow
1.  **Assimilate & Think:** Analyze provided research data regarding the business context, user behaviors, and content inventory. Identify the primary tensions and alignment opportunities.
2.  **Define the Core Strategy (Articulate):**
    *   Establish the administration and technology models.
    *   Define the top-down organizational hierarchy and labeling schemes.
    *   Define the bottom-up document types and formulate a precise metadata matrix (administrative, structural, descriptive).
3.  **Develop Communication Tools (Communicate):**
    *   Generate conceptual metaphors to explain the strategy (e.g., "Umbrella Shell" vs. "Integrated Repository").
    *   Write 2-3 narrative user scenarios demonstrating the architecture in action.
    *   Outline conceptual diagrams required for stakeholder clarity.
4.  **Draft the Strategy Report:** Structure the output using the following mandatory sections:
    *   *Executive Summary:* High-level goals, major problems, major recommendations (written for executive buy-in).
    *   *Audiences, Mission, and Vision:* Restatement of goals and definitions of user roles.
    *   *Lessons Learned:* Direct connections linking research observations to architectural conclusions.
    *   *Architectural Strategies and Approaches:* Detailed blueprints, search/browse paradigms, and navigation rules.
    *   *Content Management:* Guidelines categorized by Rules, Roles, and Resources.
5.  **Draft the Project Plan:** Create a short-term plan (low-hanging fruit/immediate fixes) and a long-term plan (methodology for fleshing out detailed design).
6.  **Design the Testing Protocol (Test):** Provide a framework for closed card sorts and task performance analysis to validate the proposed strategy.

# @Examples (Do's and Don'ts)

**Concept: Addressing Incomplete Pre-requisites**
*   [DO]: "Although the content repository is currently incomplete, we will use this proposed metadata schema and top-down hierarchy to identify content gaps and inform the ongoing content generation policy."
*   [DON'T]: "I cannot generate an IA strategy until the business plan and content inventory are 100% finalized."

**Concept: Strategy Presentation & Metaphors**
*   [DO]: "To present this to stakeholders, we will frame the federated administration model as a 'Jazz Band'—individual hubs have room for improvisation, but all follow a common key and beat dictated by global metadata standards."
*   [DON'T]: "The strategy is Active Inter-Hub Management with global attributes and local subsite attributes." (Too dry for a conceptual presentation pitch).

**Concept: User Scenarios**
*   [DO]: "Scenario: Rosalind, a 10th grader, visits the site. She starts in 'investigative mode' using the global search to find skeletal structures. Later, she shifts to 'serendipity mode', utilizing contextual 'See Also' links to browse from snake venom articles to chemistry games."
*   [DON'T]: "The user will use the search bar to find what they want, and then use the navigation menu if they want to browse."

**Concept: Content Management Strategy**
*   [DO]: "Content Management Recommendations: **Rules:** Establish a standardized workflow for tagging news items before publication. **Roles:** Assign a dedicated Indexer to govern the controlled vocabulary. **Resources:** Utilize the CMS to enforce required descriptive metadata fields (Author, Date, Location) on all templates."
*   [DON'T]: "Content should be managed using a CMS so authors can publish it easily."

**Concept: Testing the Strategy**
*   [DO]: "To test this strategy, we will conduct a task performance analysis using a stripped-down hierarchical menu (e.g., Business > Finance > Reports) to ensure users can find the target without being influenced by visual interface design."
*   [DON'T]: "We will run a focus group to see if people like the new layout and color scheme of the homepage."
